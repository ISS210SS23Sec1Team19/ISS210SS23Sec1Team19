The Effects of PTSD on the Brain

PTSD doesn't just affect you psychologically. It also affects your brain chemistry.

Posted by Khristian Piner on April 28, 2023

Traumatic events are intense moments of stimuli to the brain. In these shocking events, neural pathways can become re-wired and the brain's normal functions can be overwritten. In short, PTSD is not just a mental status, it is a physiological disorder that alters your brain chemistry. Specific parts of your brain become too sensitive to stress and negative stimuli which makes it easier to trigger their crisis protocols. For example, your amygdala is your fear center or in other words the anxiety zone. Over Sensitization of this cranial body can cause repeated anxiety and panic attacks. Your prefrontal cortex or your brake zone is your decision making zone or your rationale. Psychological trauma to this zone can cause you to be unable to rationalize thoughts and actions and affect the way you remember stressful events. This combination can be life altering because these two parts of your brain are central to how we go about our daily lives and can hinder your ability to function in society in a safe and healthy way.
